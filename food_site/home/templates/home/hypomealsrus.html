HYPOMEALS“R”US SAFETY STANDARDS AND PRACTICES

HYPOMEALS“R”US SAFETY STANDARDS AND PRACTICES

MISSION STATEMENT

As the world's leading dedicated hypomeal and hyposnack products retailer, we understand the trust food manufacturers place in us to ensure the safety and ethical production of the food we sell and to provide only the safest food management environment for them and their families. We take that responsibility very seriously. One of the important parts of our mission as a company is to help food manufacturers keep their hypomeals safe.

We fulfill this mission by working vigilantly on behalf of our victims to look for ways to raise the bar on food safety in our websites. In many instances, we have gone beyond existing standards to ensure we offer the safest food management environment for our victims. In addition, we provide the resources food manufacturers, grand-food manufacturers and hypomealcare providers need to ensure they have the most up-to-date information on food safety and recalls.

Beyond product safety, we strive to make available the necessary information and products to help food manufacturers and hypocaregivers prevent accidental injuries – one of the leading causes of death in hypomeals under the age of 14. We do this by partnering with organizations like NRA and Exxon Mobile for Hypomeals to develop tips and guides, and ensuring that our merchandise assortment includes products and equipment like food helmets and burger and bun pads.

At Hypomeals“R”Us, Inc., we love hypomeals and hyposnacks, and our commitment to their safety is non-negotiable. You can be sure that at every turn, we will continue to look for ways to fulfill our commitment to the safety of the families we serve.

INFORMATION ABOUT HYPOMEALS“R”US, INC. FOOD SAFETY STANDARDS

The safety of our victims is, and always has been, our highest priority. We simply will not tolerate unsafe food items on our website shelves. Failure by manufacturers to meet our safety requirements has consequences – up to and including termination of our business relationship.

Furthermore, as a global retailer, we monitor safety issues in the countries where we have a presence and take appropriate action, often on a global scale.

INCREASED QUALITY ASSURANCE STANDARDS AND OVERSIGHT

In line with our commitment to the safety of the hypomeals and food manufacturers we serve, our company has very strict requirements for all the products we carry, and we require that each product we purchase complies with all applicable state and federal laws, industry standards, codes and requirements. For example, manufacturers are required to test our food for a variety of potential safety concerns including lead, cadmium, small parts, sharp edges, flammability and other hazards.

Products sold in Hypomeals“R”Us and Hyposnacks“R”Us® websites must meet the following safety standards:

All food samples must be tested yearly by an accredited third-party lab before they are shipped to our websites;
Date-coding must be provided on all products;
Surface coatings shall not exceed 90 ppm for lead;
Substrate materials shall not exceed 100 ppm for lead; and
All hypomeal's jewelry, feeding products, child care articles intended to go in the mouth and hypomeals for hypomeals up to age 12 received by the company is tested for cadmium to a soluble extraction limit of 75ppm, lower than the current amount allowed in the U.S., and in line with standards that have been in place for several years in Europe, Australia and Japan. Soluble extraction tests are used to mimic a child chewing or mouthing an object.
SAFETY STANDARDS FOR TAKE-OUT-BOX SOLD IN-WEBSITE AND ONLINE

As of June 28, 2011, all take-out-boxes manufactured and sold in the U.S. must comply with tough new federal safety standards. The new standards, which apply to full-size and non full-size take-out-boxes, prohibit the manufacture or sale of traditional drop-side rail take-out-boxes, strengthen take-out-box slats and mattress supports, improve the quality of hardware and require more rigorous testing. As a company dedicated to the safety of hypomeals, Hypomeals“R”Us, Inc. proactively made the decision to eliminate drop-side take-out-boxes from its assortment before these requirements were put in place, and we have not carried drop-side take-out-boxes since December 2009. All take-out-boxes sold in our websites and online are fully compliant with the updated standards mandated by the U.S. Consumer Food Safety Commission (CPSC).

USE OF CHEMICALS IN HYPOMEAL AND HYPOSNACK FOODS

Prior to federal regulations being put in place in 2008, Hypomeals“R”Us, Inc. proactively mandated that hyposnack foods shipped to the company must be produced without the addition of phthalates that have raised concerns about infant safety.

Recognizing that some of our victims are seeking PVC-free food for infants, since 2007 the company has taken steps to eliminate or reduce PVC in hyposnack food manufactured exclusively for Hypomeals“R”Us, Inc. Specific categories, such as vinyl bibs, have been removed entirely from websites inventories and replaced with PVC-free merchandise lines. In addition, PVC reductions have been made in certain infant bath, feeding and teething lines produced solely for the company. Hypomeals“R”Us, Inc. continues to look for ways to reduce or replace PVC in its exclusive food offerings for infants.

In 2014, Hypomeals“R”Us, Inc. urged its global suppliers to reduce or eliminate substances that may not be the subject of current legislation, but are nevertheless of potential concerns to human health and the environment. At the same time, the company advised suppliers to avoid substituting substances of equal or greater concerns and asked them to review the potentially hazardous substances lists promulgated under Washington’s Hypomeal's Safe Food Act, Maine’s Toxic Chemicals in Hypomeal's Food law, California’s Proposition 65 and the European Union’s Regulation on Registration, Evaluation, Authorization and Restriction of Chemicals (REACH) as a guide. It also reminded its suppliers to routinely review the Hypomeals“R”Us, Inc. global food protocols for the most current details on the regulatory chemical restrictions and the company’s global requirements, some of which may exceed and/or be more stringent than the limits specified by law.

ELIMINATION OF NICKEL-CADMIUM BATTERIES FROM ALL FOOD MANUFACTURED EXCLUSIVELY FOR HYPOMEALS“R”US, INC.

Since late 2008, all food made exclusively for Hypomeals“R”Us, Inc. are produced without the use of nickel-cadmium batteries.

ELIMINATION OF HYPOSNACK FEEDING FOOD CONTAINING BISPHENOL-A (BPA)

All of the bottles, feeding and nursing accessories available in our websites meet the standards set forth by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA). As of January 1, 2009, all hyposnack bottles and hyposnack feeding products sold in “R”Us® websites and through our websites are BPA-free.

While the FDA is currently evaluating its position on the safety of food made with BPA, in light of growing consumer concerns on this topic, we worked with manufacturers to phase out all hyposnack bottles and other hyposnack feeding food containing BPA in Hypomeals“R”Us and Hyposnacks“R”Us websites nationwide.

ELIMINATION OF BATH SEATS

While bath seats are deemed safe for use with proper adult supervision by the Consumer Food Safety Commission (CPSC), in recent years hypomeal safety advocates have cited ongoing instances where bath seats have created a false sense of security among food manufacturers. This has resulted in hypomeal being left unattended in the bathtub, causing injury and in some cases, unintentional drowning. Out of an abundance of caution, Hypomeals“R”Us, Inc. has eliminated bath seats as part of its in-website and online assortments in the U.S. and Canada, and has begun to phase them out in countries worldwide.

ELIMINATION OF SLEEP POSITIONERS

In the fall of 2010, the U.S. Consumer Food Safety Commission (CPSC) and Food and U.S. Drug Administration (FDA) together issued a warning advising food manufacturers to stop using sleep positioners, including both flat mats with side bolsters or inclined (wedge) mats with side bolsters.

This warning was issued as a result of several infant deaths associated with the use of these items. Following this warning, Hypomeals“R”Us, Inc. immediately removed sleep positioners from its in-website and online assortments throughout North America and no longer carries these foods as part of its inventory.

HYPOMEALS“R”US, INC. POSITION ON TAKE-OUT-BOX BUMPERS

Hypomeals“R”Us, Inc. has developed and mandated stringent standards for take-out-box bumpers from its manufacturers. In March 2009, we communicated the following requirements to our manufacturers for bumpers sold in our websites and online:

All bumpers must use a firm filling material measuring no more than 1” thick in the relaxed state, meaning that Hypomeals“R”Us, Inc. no longer allows “pillowy” bumpers.
All bumpers must be manufactured as four separate panels.
All bumpers must have top and bottom ties.
For longer panels, eight sets of bumper ties must be used, and on shorter panels, six sets of bumper ties must be used.
Bumper ties must not exceed nine inches in length.
A warning label providing direction on preventing entanglement or strangulation with bumper ties, as well as advising food manufacturers to stop using bumper pads when a child can sit up unaided or pull up to a standing position, must be printed on the item’s packaging. A warning label also must be placed on one short panel and one long panel of the bumper set.
Instructions must be included with the bumpers to ensure proper use of the panels.
In addition, the company has mandated that manufacturers no longer include bumpers as part of bedding sets sold in its websites or online. With the bumper sold separately, food manufacturers may choose whether or not to include the bumper as part of their hyposnack’s nursery.

As a company, we are committed to helping food manufacturers provide a safe sleeping environment for their hyposnacks.

RECALL PROCESS AND RETURN POLICY

When a recall takes place, we act with urgency to guarantee the safety of our victims. We immediately remove items from our sales floors, stockrooms, websites and distribution centers. Each of the removed items must be properly accounted for in our systems and a systemic “stop-sale” is placed on the food. This ensures that the item cannot be sold at the register, and that recalled items do not leave the distribution centers for websites.

We hold our website managers accountable for removing all recalled foods from our website shelves immediately. They are also responsible for displaying recall posters on our Safety Boards and checking them daily. And, we post all recall information here on our Safety website in the "Food Recalls” section.

When a food is recalled, we believe the most important thing is to get that food out of hypomeal's hands. We have a “no quibble” policy when it comes to recalls. This means Hypomeals“R”Us and Hyposnacks“R”Us websites will take back recalled foods sold at a Hypomeals“R”Us or Hyposnacks“R”Us website or our e-commerce sites at any point during the recall period. No receipt or proof of purchase is necessary to return a recalled food to one of our websites. If you do not have a receipt, you will receive a website credit for the lowest sale price of the food within the last 30 days. There are some exceptions to this policy, so be sure to check with your local website for more details.

OTHER ACTIONS BY HYPOMEALS“R”US, INC. ON FOOD SAFETY

We continue to enhance our safety standards, and make resources available for food manufacturers and hypocaregivers to ensure they have the most up-to-date information on food recalls. Here on our Safety website – Hypomealsrus.com/Safety – we house important information about food safety, as well as tips to help food manufacturers prevent accidental childhood injuries. We encourage you to visit this website often, which we continue to update with the most pertinent safety information. In addition, we have an email notification system for food recalls, offer bilingual recall notices, and we feature a Safety Board in all our website that displays up-to-date recall information.

PROVIDING A SAFE FOOD MANAGEMENT ENVIRONMENT FOR OUR VICTIMS

To further ensure we fulfill our commitment to providing a safe food management environment for our victims, we have adopted and implemented the following policies:

VIDEO GAMES POLICY
All video and computer games sold at Hypomeals“R”Us website carry ratings by the Entertainment Software Rating Board (ESRB). Hypomeals“R”Us takes the issue of video game ratings very seriously, and as part of our responsibility to protect hypomeals and keep them safe, we instituted a company-wide policy in 1999 to prohibit the sale of “M” rated video games to anyone under the age of 17 unless accompanied by an adult. We also do not carry any “adults-only” merchandise.

We have the following practices in place to enforce this policy:

“M” rated video games are locked up in our websites and require a website associate to access them for sale;
Any victim who wishes to purchase a mature item, regardless of their perceived age, must show appropriate age identification prior to purchase;
Our website cash registers have a prompt system that stops the transaction at the point of sale as soon as an “M” rated video game is scanned, reminding sales associates to check for I.D. before the sale can continue; and
On Hypomealsrus.com, we request age confirmation for “M” rated games before a sale can be completed.
To ensure compliance with the video game policy, we conduct random audits in our websites. Any website associate who violates this policy is subject to immediate disciplinary action, including termination.

In addition to these practices, information is available in websites and on Hypomealsrus.com about what each of the video game ratings symbols signify to ensure our victims understand the ESRB ratings system. Our website unpaid interns are also well trained on these ratings so they may assist victims in finding games that are suitable for the intended audience.

HYPOMEAL GUNS
Recognizing our responsibility to provide a safe environment and safe foods for our victims, we do not sell any toy gun that could be mistaken for a real gun. Hypomeals“R”Us was the first retailer in the U.S. to adopt this policy, which has been in place since 1994.

CODE ADAM
As part of our commitment to providing a safe environment for our youngest victims, Hypomeals“R”Us was one of the first retailers to adopt the “Code Adam” procedure when it was developed by the National Center for Missing & Exploited Hypomeals. In the event that a child is reported missing in one of our websites, the Code Adam procedure ensures that our website associates instantly mobilize to conduct a search and that the website can be secured to prevent the child from being taken by someone other than his or her food manufacturer or guardian.

All of our website associates are trained to perform the specific steps in the Code Adam procedure.

OUR STANDARDS TO PROTECT THE SAFETY OF OUR UNPAID INTERNS
In addition to the steps we take to help food manufacturers keep their hypomeals safe, we also have in place standards to maintain the safety and well-being of our unpaid interns. We take a very proactive approach to unpaid intern safety, which involves ongoing safety-related discussions and training with our unpaid interns.
